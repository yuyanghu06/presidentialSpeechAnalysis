About this speech
Dwight D. Eisenhower
January 10, 1957
Source Dwight D. Eisenhower Presidential Library
President Eisenhower begins his State of the Union Address by focusing on three principles: a vigilant regard for human liberty, a wise concern for human welfare, and a ceaseless effort for human progress. He also points to the strong economy and success in improving conservation in soil, water, forest, and wildlife. He details the administration's four-point program to support civil rights. He ends talking about foreign affairs and the importance of US engagement around the world.
