About this speech
Dwight D. Eisenhower
January 21, 1957
Source Dwight D. Eisenhower Presidential Library
Known as the Price of Peace, President Dwight Eisenhower's second Inaugural Address focused on Communism as a divisive force. He declares the purpose of the United States to build peace with justice and work for freedom, and he cautions against isolationism.
