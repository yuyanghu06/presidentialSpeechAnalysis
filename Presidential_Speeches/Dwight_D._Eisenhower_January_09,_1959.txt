About this speech
Dwight D. Eisenhower
January 09, 1959
Source Dwight D. Eisenhower Presidential Library
The threat of communism, maintaining peace, and the importance of freedom occupy much of President Eisenhower's focus in this State of the Union Address. He outlines some of the costs of maintaining US security, including the dollar amount of modern weapons. He also points to the importance of a strong economy and the role of Congress in determining federal spending.
