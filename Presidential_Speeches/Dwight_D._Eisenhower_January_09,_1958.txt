About this speech
Dwight D. Eisenhower
January 09, 1958
Source Dwight D. Eisenhower Presidential Library
President Eisenhower begins this State of the Union Address by focusing on communism imperialism and the threat it poses to US security and economic well-being. He then lists eight items that require action including defense reorganization, mutual aid and trade, scientific cooperation, and education and research.
