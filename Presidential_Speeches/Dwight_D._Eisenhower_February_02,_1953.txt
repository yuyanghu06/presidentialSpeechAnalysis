About this speech
Dwight D. Eisenhower
February 02, 1953
Source Dwight D. Eisenhower Presidential Library
In his first State of the Union Address, President Eisenhower discusses the country's foreign policy, the threat of Communism, and the Korean War. He connects economic health to the ability to maintain an adequate military force. He pledges to balance the budget, eliminate the annual deficit, and work towards making the government more efficient. He also touches on natural resources, labor disputes, and civil rights.
